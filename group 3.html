What were the consequences of Adwa?

  • The Treaty of Addis Ababa, signed in October 1896, officially recognized Ethiopia's sovereignty and marked the only instance of a European power (Italy) formally recognizing an African state's independence during the era of colonialism. Adwa's victory helped to preserve Ethiopia's independence and solidified its status as a sovereign nation. The battle also served as a warning to other European powers that the scramble for Africa was not without risk, and that the colonized could and would resist.

Individuals that played a crucial role in the victory of adwa

  1. Emperor Menelik II: The leader of the Ethiopian forces, he played a crucial role in uniting the country and leading its defense.

  2. Empress Taytu: She led a contingent of women soldiers and was a key figure in the war effort.


  3. Ras Makonnen Wolde Mikael: A skilled military leader, he commanded a significant portion of the Ethiopian army.


  4. Ras Alula Aba Nega: A respected commander who played a vital role in the battle's success.


  5. Fitawrari Habtegiyorgis Dinegde: A renowned general, he was known for his military skills and bravery.
